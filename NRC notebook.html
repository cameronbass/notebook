Update

Believe it or not, I think my little brother's idea would actually be pretty cool. I have decided to start thsi as my project.

So, in order to figure out what actions the robot must take we must find what is needed to make french toasdt. Below are the steps needed for a human.

  1. crack some eggs into a bowl and whisk them
  2. dip a slice of bread into the eggs and throw it onto a grittle
  3. when the egg on the bread is cooked, serve on a plate.

For a robot it must complete the smae actions, but unlike humans it needs more descriptions. The list items are rather vague. Also, in order for the robot to be applicable for the NRC Robot Construction event, the robot must make one peice every ten minutes. In order to achieve this a set of instruction that are more detailed must be set into place. This will help me to know which parts I will need in my desighn.

  1. Once activated, eggs will have been cracked into a bowl by the operator and a stack of bread will be present in a certain spot.
  2. the robot msut take a piece of bread from the pile and put it into the bowl of whisked eggs. (If I have time I might add an automatic whicker as well.
  3. butter a grittle
  4. move the bread around in order to get some egg soaked into it
  5. Take this piece of bread out of the machine and place it onto a griddle
  6. wait a few minutes and flip the toast and wait a few more minutes before taking the toast off of the griddle and place it onto a plate

The only step that would need to be clarified from here is how long to keep the bread on the griddle. I wouldn't want to be serving raw eggs.

To find the amount of time it takes I am going to conduct an experiment next weekend to see how long it takes to cook french toast. This will decide whether the project will meet the ten minute requirement and how long the robot must wait to flip and serve the toast.
